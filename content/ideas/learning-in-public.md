Publishing notes *while* they are half-formed changes the incentive
structure: the cost of being visibly wrong is small and immediate, the cost
of being invisibly wrong is large and deferred.

A garden makes this safe because [maturity is
labeled](digital-gardens-are-not-blogs) — a `seedling` visibly asks not to be
judged as a conclusion.

Open question: does an agent-tended garden weaken this? If the agent polishes
everything at capture time, the "thinking out loud" texture disappears. Maybe
capture should stay rough by default and polish should be a deliberate
[tending](tend-small-and-often) action.
