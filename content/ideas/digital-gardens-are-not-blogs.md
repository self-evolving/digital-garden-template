A blog is a stream: entries are finished, dated, and slowly sink. A garden is
a space: notes are alive, revisited, and get *better* with age instead of
staler.

The practical consequences:

- **Notes are updated in place.** The edit history lives in git, not in a
  trail of "UPDATE:" banners.
- **Maturity is explicit.** A [seedling](tend-small-and-often) makes a claim
  it hasn't earned yet, and says so via its `status`.
- **Navigation is associative.** You arrive somewhere by following links and
  [topic maps](../topics/gardening-practice), not by scrolling a timeline.

The stream still exists here — it's the [diary](../diary/), which records
*when* things happened. The garden records *what you currently think*.
